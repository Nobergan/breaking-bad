What is "Breaking Bad" about?

Walter White, an ordinary high school chemistry teacher, learns that he has lung cancer in the third stage, and that he should live at best two years left. Deciding that he has nothing to lose, Walt intends to go all out. He lives in New Mexico with his teenage son with cerebral palsy and his wife. To provide a secure future for his family after his death, Walter enters the territory of crime and production drugs.

Together with his former student from the school, Mr. White begins to cook and sell methamphetamine, and in production popular in local drug circles, a desperate chemistry teacher reaches great heights. The series is about the impact of a terminal diagnosis on the average middle-class worker, and also shows how this diagnosis changes his morality transforms from a modest person into a real drug lord.
